<b> RESEARCH STATEMENT </b>

Dr. Meena Rittiruam is a computational materials scientist specializing in the integration of Density Functional Theory (DFT), Machine Learning (ML), and high-performance computing for accelerated catalyst discovery. His research focuses on developing data-driven frameworks that combine quantum-mechanical simulations with artificial intelligence to efficiently screen, predict, and optimize advanced catalytic materials for sustainable energy and environmental applications. His work has contributed to the development of machine-learning-accelerated DFT methodologies for complex catalytic systems, particularly high-entropy alloys (HEAs), where vast compositional spaces challenge conventional computational approaches. By integrating first-principles calculations, active-site modeling, feature engineering, interpretable machine learning, and high-throughput screening, his research enables rapid identification of promising catalysts and provides fundamental insights into structure–property relationships. Current research interests include AI-guided catalyst design for hydrogen evolution, oxygen evolution, oxygen reduction, carbon dioxide reduction, nitrogen reduction, and nitric oxide reduction reactions. His long-term vision is to establish a digital catalyst discovery platform that integrates quantum chemistry, machine learning, and autonomous materials screening to accelerate the development of next-generation catalysts for carbon-neutral and sustainable technologies.
